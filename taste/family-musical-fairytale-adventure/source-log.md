## Observations

### Repeated Patterns

- Story and design are tied early: visual development, storyboarding, layout, animation, look development, and lighting all reinforce story rather than existing as separate decoration. [S01-S06]
- Appeal is not only cute design; it is clarity of shape, readable expression, emotional framing, and materials that support character. [S01, S03, S06]
- Musical sequences work best when movement, choreography, lyrics, and story are developed together; the song carries world rules and character pressure. [S08-S10]
- Magic is strongest when grounded in emotional or cultural logic: family roles, home, architecture, craft, food, nature, music, or memory. [S07, S08]
- Camera and layout choices are clarity tools: point of view, dynamic composition, staging, and continuity decide whether the audience understands the emotional beat. [S02, S04]
- Lighting and color should direct attention and mark tone shifts; glowing elements should come from story objects, not generic particles. [S01, S05]
- Performance requires specific body detail: anticipation, weight, secondary action, follow-through, rhythmic motion, and small gesture nuance. [S03, S09]

### Exceptions

- This is not a license to copy Walt Disney Animation Studios, Pixar, Disney Princess films, Encanto, Wish, Frozen, Moana, Tangled, or any existing Disney character/world.
- Recent Disney Animation films vary widely in rendering, cultural setting, aspect ratio, humor level, music style, and character design. The taste should remain craft-based, not film-specific.
- Some Disney sources concern feature-film workflows. For 9:16 AI video, compress the same principles into fewer characters, simpler props, and stronger first-frame readability.
