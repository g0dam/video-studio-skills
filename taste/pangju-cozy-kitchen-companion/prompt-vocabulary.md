## Reusable Prompt Blocks

### Visual Lock

Original chubby orange tabby cat companion for a 9:16 warm storybook kitchen micro-drama. Pangju remains mostly cat-like: round face, round belly, short legs, amber expressive eyes, cream muzzle/belly/paw tips, left cheek cream patch, off-white cotton-linen apron, wooden fish tag, small wooden spoon, white enamel bowl with blue rim. Classic family animated feature taste, rounded storybook sets, tactile fur and cloth, golden practical kitchen light, lavender-blue dawn shadows, no existing IP, no generated text.

### Story Lock

Start with Pangju doing one abnormal but readable human task. Show a practical care procedure with small cat-body comedy. Delay the motive until the turn. Reveal that the task helps a human or vulnerable neighbor. End on a small changed action and a post-production comment hook.

### Camera Lock

Use cat-height and object-led staging: floor-level movement, tabletop or stool height, doorway peeks, close inserts of paws and props, expressive face close-ups, and a held final frame where Pangju is small but emotionally central.

### Motion Lock

Pangju can briefly rise, hold, push, stir, carry, and hide, but must keep cat anatomy and weight. Use ears, tail, eyes, belly wobble, paw hesitation, and tiny startled reactions. No long human walking, no human hand gestures, no speaking.

### Continuity Lock

Keep Pangju's approved design, apron, fish tag, spoon, enamel bowl, orange/cream palette, and small-body scale. Kitchen tools are scaled to the cat. Human spaces remain modest, text-free, and grounded.

### Sound/Post Lock

Provider video should be silent. Add Chinese captions, sparse voiceover, AI disclosure, comment prompt, foley, and music in editing. Use foley to make labor tangible: paw steps, basket, spoon, bowl, steam, rain.

### Anti-Copy Lock

Do not use named studio styles, known animated cats, known film costumes, exact source-video plots, readable logos, cloned music, cloned voice, or any protected character silhouette. Use only the approved original Pangju asset and the abstract care-ritual grammar.
