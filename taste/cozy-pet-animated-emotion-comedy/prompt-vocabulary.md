# Prompt Vocabulary: Life-Observation Companion Comedy

- camera: behavior-plus-question hook, companion-level POV, attention-guided close-up, expressive face close-up, tactile detail insert, human/context reaction shot, gentle push-in, low domestic camera, wider reset after emotional turn
- motion: body freeze, tiny reach, hesitant touch, head tilt, ear or tail reaction, blink before recognition, shoulder drop, small retreat, careful approach, human softening to companion level, touch that changes meaning
- color: warm practical room light, cool shadow base, one story-specific accent color, glossy eye catchlights, soft rim light, cozy but not one-note palette, emotional glow only when motivated
- texture: fur or feathers, skin softness, fabric weave, paper edge, ceramic or wood surface, soft plastic, floor texture, bedding or cushion, household wear, no readable text inside generated frames
- sound: room tone, soft steps, object contact, cloth rustle, floor creak, breath pause, small impact, gentle family-comedy cue, restrained chime for meaning shift, post-produced dialogue
- editing: behavior hook, practical misread, attention cut, tactile clue, reaction beat, inner-meaning insert, return through touch or gaze, quiet recognition beat, warm changed ending
- performance: appealing feature-animation acting, clear silhouette, readable anticipation, restrained squash-and-stretch, follow-through, sincere reaction timing, dignity-preserving comedy, earned cuteness
- story: real-life household behavior, hidden companion logic, human misread, role reversal, ordinary detail treated as emotionally important, familiar life moment becomes warm discovery, funny because it feels true

## Negative Vocabulary

- avoid: Disney style, Pixar style, Zootopia style, named studios, named animated characters, copied source-video plot, copied source-video objects, copied dialogue, copied pet design, copied shot order, readable subtitles generated in-frame, logos, brand text, random unmotivated magic, overbusy household clutter, cruel gag, meme-only cuteness
- common AI failures: generic cute-pet pose with no story question, human hands on animal, unstable species markings, object continuity errors, unreadable fake captions, too-dark room, plastic toy texture, overlarge eyes without anatomy, warped human face, inconsistent room geography, fantasy insert disconnected from the emotional logic, excessive sparkle

## Reusable Prompt Blocks

### Visual Lock

Original life-observation companion comedy, family-friendly animated short, familiar household behavior with hidden emotional logic, one original companion character, one newly designed emotional focus detail, tactile domestic environment, warm practical light against cool shadow, appealing feature-animation acting, readable staging, no existing IP, no readable text, no logos, no copied source-video props or story beats.

### Story Lock

Start from a real-life companion behavior people recognize at home. Give it a hidden emotional logic. Let the human or environment misread it first, then let the audience understand the companion's point of view through behavior, attention, and reaction. The ending should feel like a warm insight about ordinary life, not a replay of a reference script.

### Transition Lock

Move between reality and inner meaning through a motivated bridge: eye-line, sound cue, touch, reflection, body freeze, or tactile contact with the current story's original focus detail. Keep imagined or exaggerated moments short, emotionally specific, and clearly connected to the real scene.

### Motion Lock

Use small, precise acting: anticipation before a tiny action, a pause before recognition, follow-through on loose body parts or materials, and a softened return after the human/context understands. The companion remains species-specific; the humor comes from point of view, not humanized randomness.

### Continuity Lock

Keep companion identity, markings, body weight, emotional posture, room geography, and the story's focus detail consistent. Do not import the reference videos' exact pet, human, object, room, transformation, dialogue, or shot sequence. Add precise subtitles and end-card text in post-production only.
