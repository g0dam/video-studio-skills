## Observations

### Repeated Patterns

- Both sources convert familiar household behavior into a second emotional meaning. The reusable pattern is "ordinary behavior has hidden companion logic", not the literal behavior or object. [S01, S02]
- The strongest hook is a behavior question: the viewer sees a companion's attention, reaction, or hesitation and wants to know what it means. [S01, S02]
- The emotional payoff is a re-read: a human, context, or audience first interprets the behavior practically, then understands a warmer point of view. [S01, S02, R01]
- Scene shifts work best when motivated by attention, touch, sound, reflection, or tactile contact. The bridge matters more than the specific source prop. [S01, S02]
- The appealing animation quality comes from craft rather than IP imitation: clear staging, readable silhouette, precise anticipation, tactile materials, warm/cool lighting, and sincere reaction timing. [S01, S02, R01]
- Animal or companion performance should preserve body logic while allowing stylized expression. Emotion should be carried by attention, body weight, secondary motion, and reaction timing. [S01, S02]
- The usable reference is craft-level only: life observation, hidden emotional logic, attention-driven transition, feature-animation appeal, and dignity-preserving humor. [S01, S02, R01]

### Exceptions
