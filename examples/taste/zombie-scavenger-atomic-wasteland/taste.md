# Zombie Scavenger Atomic Wasteland

## Essence

A compact atom-punk zombie fable built from collision rather than lore: seaside luxury ruins, western robot iconography, a zombie cleanup job, and an impossible romance with a mannequin. The style works because every shot keeps a readable job, silhouette, or emotional signal in frame. It uses AI-friendly non-human performance as the core character language: LED expressions, mechanical pauses, clean body poses, and deadpan timing carry the feeling instead of fragile human facial acting.

## Best For

- 45-second to 4-minute AI shorts with a complete genre arc.
- Weird-western, atom-punk, post-apocalyptic comedy-romance, robot fable, or odd-job sci-fi premises.
- No-dialogue or low-dialogue shorts where emotion must come from silhouette, props, gesture, and music.
- Concepts that need cinematic spectacle while staying robust for AI generation.

## Narrative Grammar

- Hook: open with visual contradiction and scale. A beautiful seaside resort/pool world is already broken, then the worker-hero appears as if this impossible cleanup is routine.
- Build: treat the premise as a job, not exposition. The hero crosses resort interiors, dead streets, shops, and zombie pressure zones while tools, mount/vehicle logic, and LED expressions define personality.
- Turn: shift from violent cleanup into deadpan tenderness. The robot recognizes "love" through found social rituals: mannequin gaze, date-like staging, gift/gesture, sunset companionship.
- Payoff: end on an object or ritual that reframes the whole short as loneliness, not just zombie spectacle. The sampled tail imagery pushes the romance toward escape/space fantasy, while secondary sources describe a gift-like romantic closure.
- Common motifs: ocean horizon, luxury villa/pool, red sequence/title typography, robot cowboy silhouette, LED face, ostrich/mount gag, shopping street ruins, mannequin body, sunset bench, pod/window view of Earth.

## Visual Grammar

- Composition: ultra-wide 2.33:1 frames with strong horizontal geography: ocean, balcony, streets, shopfronts, windows, and orbital horizon. Hero shots are centered and silhouette-readable; action shots stage bodies diagonally across dead streets.
- Lens and shot size: wide and medium-wide shots carry most story information. Close-ups are reserved for LED-face emotion, mannequin reaction, hands/tools, and comic object details.
- Camera movement: use smooth AI-friendly moves: aerial-feeling opens, slow push from environment into character, side-tracking action, reveal from prop/window to horizon, and short action-follow moves. Avoid chaotic handheld camera.
- Blocking: the hero enters, assesses, performs one clean action, pauses, then moves to the next tableau. Zombies act as mass pressure and texture, not as complex individual acting. Mannequin scenes should be still, frontal, and ritualized.
- Texture and materials: polished resort glass, cracked concrete, sunlit pool water, rusted metal, black leather, chrome weapon/tool edges, dirty signage, plastic mannequin skin, zombie residue, LED pixels, orbital glass.

## Edit Rhythm

- Cut density: fast enough to deliver a full 3.5-minute arc. Automated scene detection on the 213.5s sample produced roughly 90 high-contrast change candidates, so plan for about 2-3 seconds per visual idea on average, with slower holds for romance.
- Transition logic: cut by task progression, geography reveal, prop discovery, LED reaction, title-card punch, or emotional reversal. Each shot should do one job.
- Silence or pause rules: after impossible details, hold briefly. The LED smile, angry face, sad face, or mannequin stare needs a beat before the next spectacle shot.

## Color, Light, and Sound

- Palette: cyan ocean and sky, hard white sunlight, sun-bleached orange, rust red, dirty teal, black leather, faded resort colors, high-contrast red typography, LED green/red/white expression accents.
- Light sources: hard coastal sun, sunset backlight, interior window light, practical resort/store light, muzzle/electric flashes, LED face panel, orbital window glow.
- Music: should feel cinematic and slightly romantic without becoming trailer noise. Electronic grit fits machinery and combat; a warmer melodic lift is needed once the mannequin romance appears.
- Natural sound: surf/wind, glass and water impact, metal servo movement, weapon handling, street debris, zombie crowd mass, ostrich/mount footfalls, cloth/leather movement, pod ambience.
- Voice: safest as no dialogue or almost no dialogue. Let title cards and visual rituals carry meaning. Bilibili metadata exposed no subtitle track in this pass.

## Performance Direction

- Gesture scale: deliberate, readable, slightly theatrical. Robot movement should be competent and weighty, with small awkward pauses that create comedy.
- Eye behavior: replace eye acting with LED symbols, face color, head tilt, hand hesitation, and body orientation toward the mannequin or threat.
- Emotional temperature: lonely competence first, tenderness second. Comedy stays dry; horror stays stylized; romance is sincere enough to avoid parody.

## Platform Behavior

- Aspect ratios: source grammar is ultra-wide/cinematic. For vertical reposts, keep the robot, mount/vehicle, and companion object in the central crop; do not rely on edge-only details.
- First-frame rule: communicate the share hook visually: beautiful world already ruined, strange worker-hero, immediate zombie/task pressure.
- Caption/subtitle behavior: captions should sell the premise, not narrate the plot. Red title cards can mark chapter/genre energy, but tiny in-world text should not be required for comprehension.
- Loop or ending behavior: finish on a ritual object, companion pose, or travel/escape image that makes the viewer reinterpret the action as a loneliness story.

## Prompt Implications

- Use: a clear worker role, one non-human lead, one odd mount/vehicle/tool system, one abandoned leisure or consumer space, one object of affection, stylized zombie pressure, red graphic punctuation, and repeatable production design.
- Avoid: copying the exact robot cowboy plus ostrich plus mannequin package; overloading each shot with new monsters; realistic human romantic acting; long voiceover exposition; generic purple/blue cyberpunk; chaotic crowd fights.
- Render risks: animal feet, zombie crowd limbs, weapon/tool continuity, LED face symbol drift, reflective metal, glass/pool water, mannequin anatomy, fast dance contact, small signage, red text legibility, ultra-wide-to-vertical crop loss.
